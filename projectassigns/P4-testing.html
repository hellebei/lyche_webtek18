Testing document

To get both qualitative and quantitative test results we created an user test and a Google Form (link here) to test our web page. We wanted to test different aspects of the page's user-friendliness and usability. We sent the form to friends, fellow students and family with a note saying that the page is aimed at Google Chrome, and recommended them to use this web browser (this is included in the form as well). We also tested on one of our contact people as well. This is to get valuable information from the people who run the restaurant. For the live testing we developed a semistructured user test with some warm up-questions, a couple of tasks to understand the users conseptual model of the web page and to test the navigation structure. We then asked them about the general layout and if they had other comments. Here's the document we used for the live testing:

Other comments and feedback:

Testing audience

Our web page is primary aimed at students and this is the biggest part of our user group.

From the google form we have these key numbers about who we reached:

We where able to get 21 people to anwser the Google form. Here we sendt the testsheet to friends who we know have heard of Lyche before, and also posted our sheet in our class facebook pages. These people are the intended users for the page so therefore it was resonable to contact people this way.

From the people who anwsered has 57% of them not ordered a table at Lyche before, but 43% have. Here we see that the numbers are quite even and this is an intresting observation.

We also found out that 90.5% of the people who anwsered are students, and only 9.5% are working full time. Although everyone who anwsered was in the age group between 18 and 29.

We also did a live testing on 4 people. Here 3 had a non-technical background in that way that they do not study something technical. And one of them had a technical background. They where all students, and they had all heard about Lyche before.

Testing platforms

We wanted to test different platforms to make sure the site is responsive and looked the same across all platforms. From the live testing we did, where we also able to get information of how the page worked on different platforms. We did one live testing with a mac, one with using a PC, one with using a surface book and one with using a phone. All of the live testings were done using Google Chrome.

Feedback summary

Live testing

The live testing is incredibly important because it shows you how people use the page, and how they understand the different features of the page. We also get instantly feedback from what they do understand and what they have to trial and error to understand.

In summary, the testing showed us that the page seems easy to use. The hamburgermenu is frequently used to navigate through the page, but the scroll function was a bit harder to spot. One didn´t scroll down from the start, because the page is fully filled so the front page did not symbolize that function. Although she said that if she had been on her phone had it been more convenient to scroll down.

We got a lot of positive feedback on the design and the layout. People in generally said it was a page they would use.

Google Form, by question type:

Navigation

In summary do people find it overall easy to navigate through the webpage. From the three task we had that focused on navigation, did people mostly find the diffuculty level on the task quite low.

On the task where you should book a table did 43% say it had a diffuculty level on 1 (easy) and 48% said it had a difficulty level on 2 (quite easy). Only 2 people said it was more difficult (level 4).

On the task where you should check the menu, 62% said it was easy to navigate to (level 1). The rest was a bit spread at difficulty level 2 and 3. Where you could fill out other commets, had somebody written that the menu is quite overwhelming because everything is in the same font size. Maybe it could be set up differently, so that it would be easier to navigate in the menu page.

On finding Lyches facebook page also here did 62% say it was easy to navigate to (level 1). But also some, 9%, said it was difficult (level 5).

Usability

From the feedback of how the navigation bar worked did 95% anwser that the symbols on the navigation bar was understandable. Here we had one comment that the logo to Samf was partly covered by the scrollbar. And also that the NO/EN is a bit close to the Samfundet logo so that one hit the wrong link and ended up on Samfundeds page when she was actually trying to get the page in English.

Design

From other comments section did we get the feedback that it was aesthetically pleasing, with good and relative pictures

Content

We got feedback that the page containd just the right amount of informative text, and with relative pictures to go with the text so that it was easier to understand and navigate to. And also pleasing to look at.

Our response

From the feedback we got from the users are there several things we could improve.

We could implement a down-key arrow on the front page to signalize that there's more content below the slide show.

The samfundet homepage should open in another tab.

Possibly move age limits to be mentioned somewhere in connection to the opening hours.

We also got the comment of lack of feedback when you choose language. Here we could have the selected language more bold.

Self-reflection

By testing the page on people other than us, are we able to get input about things that we dont see. By making the page, and using so much time on it, do we get very used to the page, and sometimes a little bit blind. Therefore does testing on other people open up for alot of different views. For example does thing that we feel are quite convinitent, is maybe not as convinient for the users. This is for example the scroll function, which was something we thought was very convinient, but the user did not.
